Sushant Singh Rajput

About Shows Films More

Sushant Singh Rajput was an Indian Bollywood film actor. He started his career with tv serials. Then he made his Bollywood debut in Kai Po Che! in 2013. His perfomance on the filmy biopic of Indian Cricket Team captain M. S. Dhoni caught the eyes of cinema-lovers. Unexpectedly, he was found dead hanging from the ceiling fan in his home on 14th June, 2020. Though his fans do not believe that he comitted suicide, since he was in rising period of his filmy career, it is said that he was suffering from depression and was a victim of nepotism in the Bollywood industry.

  • Born on: Jan 21, 1986 in Maldiha, Bihar
  • Died on: Jun 14, 2020 in Bandra, Mumbai
  • Aged: 34 years old
  • Debut show: Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil (2008)

TV Career (2008-2011)

In 2008, the casting team of Balaji Telefilms saw Rajput's acting talent and offered the role of Preet Juneja in Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil. His character was killed quite early in the show, but he was such a popular character with the viewers that he was brought back for the series finale in the form of a spirit.

In 2009, Rajput began starring in Pavitra Rishta as Manav Deshmukh, a serious and mature character working as a mechanic to support his family. His work in this serial received wide appreciation, and Rajput received three major television awards for the best male actor and most popular actor.

Filmy Career (2013-2020)

In 2013, Rajput auditioned for Abhishek Kapoor's Kai Po Che! and was selected to play one of the three leads, along with Rajkumar Rao and Amit Sadh. Based on the Chetan Bhagat's novel The 3 Mistakes of My Life, the film proved to be a critical and commercial success.

His second movie was Shuddh Desi Romance in 2013, alongside Parineeti Chopra and Vaani Kapoor. Directed by Maneesh Sharma and produced by Yash Raj Fims.

Sushant's next role was in Rajkumar Hirani's 2014 film PK. Although his role was relatively minor, the film gave him the opportunity to work with Aamir Khan and Anushka Sharma.

In 2015, Rajput's sole release was Dibakar Banerjee's mystery thriller Detective Byomkesh Bakshy! in which he portrayed detective Byomkesh Bakshi, created by Bengali writer Sharadindu Bandyopadhyay

In 2016, he appeared in Neeraj Pandey's biopic M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story, in lead role of Indian cricketer Mahendra Singh Dhoni. The film was a critical and commercial success and earned him his first nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor.

In 2017, he appeared in Dinesh Vijan's Raabta, co-starring Kriti Sanon. In this film he played the character of Shiv, a warrior prince who gets reincarnated after centuries to get revenge and to get his love back.

In 2018, he appeared in Kedarnath, a love story set in the backdrop of the 2013 floods in Uttarakhand, co-starring Sara Ali Khan. He played the role of Mansoor Khan, a young Kashmiri man.

In 2019, he appeared in Abhishek Chaubey's Sonchiriya opposite Bhumi Pednekar.

Then he appeared in Nitesh Tiwari's Chhichhore opposite Shraddha Kapoor that released on 6 September 2019.

Later in 2019, he starred in Drive alongside Jacqueline Fernandez, which was released on Netflix.

In 2020, he starred in Mukesh Chhabra's remake of The Fault in Our Stars titled Dil Bechara.
